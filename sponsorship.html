Presidential Letter

Dear Sir/Madam,

Greetings from the Association of Indonesian Students (ASIS) at the University of Southern California 2018-2019. We thank you for your interest in supporting our organization for the upcoming year. Your help will enable the Indonesian community at USC to grow and for our members to develop the skills they need for the future, to have the best college experience and eventually to make their own contribution back to the greater Indonesian community. We invite you and your company to learn more about our organization through this website and to join us in fostering excellence, innovation, and growth in ASIS.

As a recognized USC student organization, ASIS aims to create a vibrant Indonesian community. With over 100 members at USC, we want to reach out to every Indonesian student and everyone who shares our interest in Indonesia and its culture. We serve as a platform to facilitate Indonesian students to network and promote awareness of Indonesian culture at USC. In reaching our goals, we have planned events for the upcoming year. Some of our events are celebrated annually, such as the fall/spring welcoming parties for current and incoming students and the Trojans Cup, the annual soccer competition participated by other Indonesian organizations throughout Los Angeles. To develop our member’s professional skills, we hope to host professional events partnering with our sponsors. We are also planning to showcase Indonesian culture at events in conjunction with USC’s International Students Assembly.

Please take the time to look through this website that lists the events we have planned for the upcoming year, and see why USC ASIS is one of the most acclaimed student organizations on campus. Our sky-high ambitions require financial support from companies such as yours. With our prominence on campus and in the Los Angeles community and our global Trojan network, we offer you sponsorship benefits for your help. By supporting ASIS, you are not only aiding future leaders, but you will also advertise your company, and have access to some of the world’s most brilliant minds.

Thank you again for your time and generosity. Please feel free to contact us with any questions via email at asis@usc.edu, or visit us online at https://www.uscasis.com/.

Sincerely,
Jason Chu
President of USC ASIS 2018-2019

Our Mission

Our mission is to create a strong Indonesian community at USC and provide all with a sense of belonging in their home away from home. We hope to create an enriching experience for each member to transition into USC, but allow students to maintain their cultural roots and heritage.

In addition, we hope to spread the Indonesian culture to our fellow Trojans. Allowing the Trojan family to experience Indonesian culture and cuisines. Not only will students build a strong network of USC alumni, but everlasting friendships that will last beyond the college experience.

Our 2018-2019 Goals
Execute Our Mission Statement
Our primary goal this year is to grow our organization and promote Indonesian culture. We plan to create many events showcasing Indonesia and many opportunities for the Trojan family to get their fix of Indonesian food. All in all, we would like to build a strong-knit community and create lifelong friendships while remembering our roots.
Foster Professional Growth and Development
Another goal is to help incoming transfers and freshmen transition into not only life at USC but also their future careers. Our plan is to continue to grow and improve our newly formed mentorship program, allowing students to have a support system for their journey. Additionally, we will host professional events, inviting guest speakers and alumni to share their wealth of knowledge and look to collaborate with our sponsors to hold corporate events.
Give Back to the Community
Finally, as an organization we would like to give back to the community and volunteer. Throughout the year we will host volunteering events that may help to make LA greener, aid the less fortunate, or take the time to share our knowledge with the younger generation. Also, we look forward to working with USC's International Student Assembly (ISA) to promote diversity on campus.
